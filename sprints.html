Kunal’s Design Sprints

Sarah for Transfer Students

We overhauled Williams College’s “Sarah” student portal so transfer students can find schedules, records, and financial-aid forms in seconds. Starting with heuristic analysis, we discovered navigation blind spots and poor information architecture on the website, a common complaint amongst students. Paper sketches and a high-contrast Figma prototype reorganised content into clear buttons grouped by their respective tasks, using Williams’ purple-and-gold palette for both branding and WCAG-AA contrast. In-class testing confirmed faster orientation and less cognitive load, thanks to Gestalt grouping and consistent breadcrumbs.

Gesture-Powered Super Mario Bros.

Our team explored replacing classic WASD controls with hand-gestures detected by MediaPipe. We mapped intuitive gestures to key presses, including Open Palm → Move Right & Thumbs-Up → Jump, and iterated via Wizard-of-Oz testing. Testers reported the scheme felt natural and more immersive, though overly sensitive custom poses were dropped to reduce error and fatigue.

A Book Catalog UI/UX Makeover

Our team rebuilt a book-catalog site into a cleaner, more discoverable, and more visually appealing website. Using Bootstrap’s grid plus custom CSS, we introduced a monochrome palette that lets colourful book covers pop, hover-responsive cards, and search + author filters. Five usability interviews praised the visual hierarchy but requested more titles that we hope to implement for future iterations. Overall, incremental, user-driven tweaks proved more impactful than a bold, flashy redesign.

Descriptify: An AI-Powered Browser Extension

To tackle missing alt-text on the web, our team designed a Chrome extension that generates AI captions when any image is clicked. Needs-gathering pinpointed “visual exclusion” as a problem on various website. Through our sketches, we created a minimal pop-up UI. The image is piped to Groq AI, where we then see a caption and progress feedback, addressing confusion spotted in early user testing. Screen-reader users now get instant, WCAG-compliant descriptions without leaving the page.
